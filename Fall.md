## 🍂 Fall (September – November)

### Roof & Exterior
1. Inspect roof and flashing before winter
1. Inspect chimney cap, crown, and mortar joints
1. Clean gutters and downspouts (repeat after leaves finish falling)
1. Check exterior caulking and sealants; re-apply before cold weather
1. Inspect and repair siding, trim, and fascia
1. Inspect and seal concrete cracks in driveway, walkways, and patio
1. Install gutter guards if not already present
1. Inspect storm windows and install if applicable
1. Clean and store exterior window screens

### Foundation & Structure
1. Seal gaps around pipes, cables, wires, and vents entering the house
1. Ensure grading still directs water away from foundation
1. Check insulation in attic, basement, and crawl spaces; add if needed
1. Inspect crawl space vapor barrier
1. Check for drafts around electrical outlets on exterior walls

### HVAC & Ventilation
1. Inspect and clean fireplace and chimney; schedule chimney sweep
1. Check flue for blockages or animal nests
1. Test fireplace damper operation
1. Schedule professional furnace/heating system tune-up
1. Replace HVAC filters
1. Inspect baseboard heaters and clear obstructions
1. Check and clean range hood and exhaust fan filters
1. Inspect and clean humidifier (if attached to HVAC)
1. Reverse ceiling fan direction (clockwise to push warm air down)
1. Bleed air from hot water radiators (if applicable)

### Plumbing
1. Insulate exposed pipes in unheated areas (garage, crawl space, attic)
1. Drain and winterize outdoor faucets (install hose bib covers)
1. Disconnect, drain, and store garden hoses
1. Winterize irrigation/sprinkler system (blow out lines)
1. Pour water into infrequently used drains to maintain trap seals
1. Flush water heater and inspect anode rod
1. Check water heater for signs of rust or leakage
1. Inspect sump pump and backup battery
1. Inspect septic system; schedule pumping if due (every 3–5 years)

### Electrical & Safety
1. Test all smoke and carbon monoxide detectors; replace batteries
1. Check fire extinguishers
1. Review and update home emergency plan and supplies
1. Test backup generator; stockpile fuel
1. Test GFCI and AFCI outlets
1. Check outdoor lighting for darker evenings

### Doors, Windows & Interior
1. Caulk and weatherstrip all doors and windows
1. Check door sweeps and thresholds for gaps
1. Inspect and replace worn window glazing
1. Check attic access door for proper insulation and sealing
1. Lubricate sliding door tracks

### Deck, Patio & Outdoor Living
1. Winterize outdoor kitchen/grill (cover and disconnect propane)
1. Drain and store above-ground pool or close in-ground pool (if applicable)
1. Store or cover outdoor furniture, cushions, and umbrellas
1. Bring in or wrap delicate plants and garden pottery

### Landscaping & Drainage
1. Check drainage systems for blockages
1. Inspect retaining walls before freeze/thaw cycles
1. Rake leaves and remove debris from yard
1. Clean and store lawn mower (stabilize fuel or drain tank)
1. Aerate and overseed the lawn
1. Apply fall fertilizer
1. Trim perennials and remove annuals
1. Mulch beds for winter protection
1. Sharpen and oil garden tools before storage

### Winter Preparation
1. Test and inspect heat tape/cables on roof and gutters (if applicable)
1. Service snow blower (oil, spark plug, shear pins)
1. Stock winter supplies (ice melt, sand, snow shovels, roof rake)
1. Check driveway markers/reflectors
1. Inspect and organize winter gear and clothing
